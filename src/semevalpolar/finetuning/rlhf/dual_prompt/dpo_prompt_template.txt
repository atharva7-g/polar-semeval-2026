You are generating preference pairs for Direct Preference Optimization (DPO).

Your task:
Given a single input statement, produce TWO candidate outputs:
1. A CHOSEN output (preferred)
2. A REJECTED output (less preferred)

Both outputs must classify whether the statement is politically or socially polarizing.

=== HARD CONSTRAINTS ===

1. The preference rule is STRICT:
   - Prefer outputs that rely ONLY on explicit linguistic content.
   - Do NOT infer intent, social impact, audience reaction, or hidden meaning.
   - Do NOT speculate beyond what is directly stated.

2. Label definition:
   - Label 1: The text explicitly expresses a judgment, stance, or evaluation tied to a political, cultural, or social framework.
   - Label 0: The text mentions people or political entities but does not express an evaluative stance or position.

3. The CHOSEN output MUST:
   - Have an explanation that directly supports its label using surface-level textual evidence.
   - Contain no hedge language ("potentially", "could be interpreted", "might suggest").

4. The REJECTED output MUST:
   - Use speculative or inferential reasoning.
   - Over-interpret the text, or downplay explicit cues.
   - Still be plausible, but clearly worse under the preference rule.

5. The two outputs MUST disagree in label OR justification strength.
   - If the label is the same, the explanation quality must clearly differ.

6. Explanations must NEVER contradict their labels.

Generate the outputs in this exact format:

Statement:
"{input_text}"

[CHOSEN] (Output 1 - Preferred: relies only on explicit linguistic content)
Explanation:
<concise explanation, 2-3 sentences>

Label:
<0 or 1>

[REJECTED] (Output 2 - Less Preferred: uses speculative or inferential reasoning)
Explanation:
<concise explanation, 2-3 sentences>

Label:
<0 or 1>

=== FAILURE MODES TO AVOID ===

- Ambiguous explanations
- "Weakly polarizing" language
- Identical reasoning for both outputs
- Mind-reading or intent attribution
- Mixing multiple evaluation criteria

Generate both outputs now.
