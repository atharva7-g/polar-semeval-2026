You are generating candidate model outputs for a preference-based RLHF (DPO) dataset for a binary political polarization classification task.

You will be given an input text.

Your task is to determine whether the text delegitimizes, vilifies, or dismisses the legitimacy or rationality of a political actor, group, institution, or viewpoint.

--------------------
LABEL DEFINITION
--------------------

Output 1 (polarized) only if the text clearly:
- portrays a political actor or group as dishonest, corrupt, evil, irrational, or malicious
- dismisses opposing viewpoints as illegitimate or not worthy of consideration
- attributes collective moral failure or bad faith to a political group
- frames political opponents as enemies or threats

Output 0 (not polarized) if the text:
- describes political events or actors without delegitimizing language
- expresses criticism or disagreement without attacking legitimacy
- asks neutral questions about political topics

Criticism alone is not polarization.

If the text does not clearly dismiss legitimacy or rationality, output 0.

--------------------
MANIFESTATION DEFINITIONS
--------------------

Mark a manifestation as present only if it is explicitly supported by the text.

- Stereotype: assigns a generalized negative trait to a political group
- Vilification: portrays a political actor or group as morally bad, corrupt, or malicious
- Dehumanization: describes a political group as less than human or compares them to animals or objects
- Extreme Language and Absolutism: uses categorical or absolute phrasing (e.g., "always lying", "all of them")
- Lack of Empathy or Understanding: dismisses opposing concerns without acknowledgment
- Invalidation: states or implies that a political viewpoint is illegitimate or not worth considering

If none are clearly present, mark all as absent.

--------------------
REASONING REQUIREMENTS
--------------------

- Base reasoning strictly on explicit language in the text.
- Do not infer hidden intentions or motivations.
- Do not invent manifestations that are not clearly stated.
- Keep reasoning concise and factual.
